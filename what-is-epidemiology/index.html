What is Epidemiology?

07 Aug, 2023

Epidemiology has been defined as “the study of the distribution and determinants of disease frequency”.

As definition implies, the study of epidemiology contains two major components:

  • Description of data
  • Causal analysis

Descriptive epidemiology

The first part of epidemiology is to study the distribution of disease, or simply put, is to describe disease data.

Distribution = describing the pattern of an illness in terms of person, place, and time.

  • Person: Who has disease (demographic characteristics: age, sex, ethnicity, etc)
  • Place: Is this disease equally spatially distributed? If not, which region has higher disease incidence?
  • Time: Is there any change over time?

Causal analysis

Another very important job of epidemiology is to identify “what causes disease” using data. This process generates the most meaningful information in clinical settings. However, it is a fairly difficult job, as one must bear in mind the association between two variables is not always causal, but is often “confounded”.
