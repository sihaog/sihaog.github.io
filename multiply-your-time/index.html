Multiply your time

26 Jul, 2023

I just watched an interesting TED Talk on “How to Multiply Your Time” because I’ve been struggling with time management and procrastination these days.

Important ideas came from this video:

Significance Calculation

Multiply your time means do significance calculation. Time management is not a logical thing to do, but an emotional thing to deal with.

You MULTIPLY your time by giving yourself the EMOTIONAL PERMISSION to spend time on things TODAY that will give you more time TOMORROW.

That reminds me of my early days at primary and secondary school, when I always got a lot of homework to do especially when it comes to weekends and holidays. I really hated these long holidays because the homework created a really huge burden for me.

Initially, I, like many of my classmates, always procrastinate on my homework. It was one Friday night when it surprisingly came to my mind that if I could just finish my homework however late that night, I would be really happy and enjoy the following weekend holiday, without stress, completely free. So, I got all my homework done that night, and have been doing that ever since. And yes, I gained and enjoyed freedom.

Now after watching that YouTube video, I finally realize that, the feeling throughout my Friday nights was an emotional advantage. I succeeded in multiplying my time on the weekends, by forcing myself spending the Friday nights on my homework.

That’s true. Time management is an emotional thing.

The FOCUS Funnel

Another point of that YouTube video is about the Focus Funnel, as shown below.

  1. Eliminating (Saying No to) things you don’t have to do.
  2. Automating the task you need to do.
  3. Delegating the tasks you need to do, by teaching others. (So that you will free yourself up.)

If the idea of significance calculation is to change our perspective, the Focus Funnel gives me a useful guide on exactly how to deal with tasks today.
